MANUAL HANDLING (100KG PROTOCOL)

Scope

This section covers the safe handling, transportation, installation, and fixing of fire doors weighing in excess of 100 kg, ensuring compliance with HSE manual handling guidance, including TEAM handling principles and the Hierarchy of Control MUST be used in all areas before any manual lifting is conducted. All manual lifts are to be over very short distances and for short periods of time. All actions follow the HSE Guidance, an annex has this in detail.

1. Personnel Requirements

A minimum of two trained operatives must be used for lifting and positioning each door. All operatives must have current manual handling training aligned with HSE MAC/TEAM guidance. The Lead Fitter will act as the coordinator for all lifting and installation activities. For doors over 100 kg, additional operatives or mechanical lifting equipment must be provided prior to work starting. Pre Site information must be assessed before manpower allocation is made.

2. Pre-Lift Assessment

Before handling any door, operatives must carry out a coordinated TEAM assessment:

2.1 Task: Confirm approximate weight (estimated at 100 kg+) and dimensions. Assess the required movements, including lifting, carrying, turning, and positioning into the aperture. Ensure the task does not exceed the safe capacity for a two-person lift.

2.2 Environment: Inspect the route from storage to installation for obstructions, uneven ground, slopes, or trip hazards. Ensure lighting is adequate and space allows safe team movement. Confirm the installation area is stable, clear of debris, and level.

2.3 Individual: All operatives involved must be fit for work, competent, and familiar with team lifting communication signals. Any operative reporting injury, fatigue, or unsuitable capability must be excluded from the lift.

2.4 Load: Doors must be assessed for: Weight distribution, Sharp edges, Balance point, Grip/handling points or need for lifting aids. Loads exceeding safe handling limits will require mechanical assistance.

3. Use of Mechanical Aids

To reduce manual handling risk, the following aids must be used wherever possible: Door trolleys or panel lifters, Lifting straps or handles, Heavy-duty dollies, Powered lifting aids (hoists, vacuum lifters, small cranes) for doors >100 kg or where access routes are restricted. Mechanical aids must be inspected prior to use and used only by trained personnel.

4. Storage and Preparation

Doors must be stored as close as practicable to the installation point to minimise carrying distance. When stored vertically, doors must be secured to prevent tipping. Only one door is to be handled at a time. The installation area is to be prepared, cleared, and stabilised before beginning the lift.

5. Lifting Procedure

5.1 Pre-Lift Briefing: The Lead Fitter will brief the team on: Lifting technique (upright lift, neutral back posture), Hand positioning and load balance, Communication and coordinated movements (stop/control signals), Route and hazards.

5.2 Team Lift: Two operatives lift the door in a controlled, synchronous manner, keeping: The door upright, The load close to the body (In the Green Zone), Movements slow and stable. The door is then carried using the most direct, obstruction-free route (Only over very short distances).

5.3 Positioning in the Aperture: One operative supports and steadies the door in position, maintaining upright stability. The second operative begins mechanical fixing, ensuring: Correct fixings used, Fixings applied in accordance with manufacturer instructions, Temporary supports or wedges remain in place until the frame is fully secured.

6. Installation & Adjustment

Once fixed, the door must be checked for: Alignment, Smooth operation, Correct gap tolerances. No supports may be removed until adjustments and checks are completed. Defects or instability must be corrected before releasing the load.

7. Heavy Doors and Insufficient Manpower

For doors exceeding 100 kg, mechanical lifting solutions must be arranged in advance (hoists, vacuum lifters, or mechanical cranes). Project Leads must ensure adequate manpower is available for all lifts. If mechanical aids or sufficient personnel are not available: Work must not proceed, The issue must be reported immediately to the Head of Installation, Work may only begin once safe lifting arrangements are in place.

8. Stopping Work

Work must be stopped immediately if: A safe team lift cannot be completed, Conditions change (e.g., wet floors, visibility reduction, obstruction), Equipment becomes damaged or defective, Any operative feels the task is unsafe.

9. Residual Risks

Risk of musculoskeletal injury from poor technique or excessive load. Risk of crushing injury if the door becomes unstable. Risk of slips/trips if routes are not properly maintained. All residual risks must be controlled through strict adherence to this RAMS section.

FIXING DOOR SETS

On an agreed date/ time with site management and with all relevant paperwork in place, the Door Sets will be securely wrapped on pallets and ready for moving to the work area. Fixing of the Doors will be done using handheld 18v drills and hand tools only. Minimum of two operatives will lift the door using the best practised manual handling techniques into the door aperture. One fitter supporting the door will prevent it from falling over whilst in position, the second fitter will mechanically fix the frame to the wall using correct fixings as trained. All doors which are above the weighted ability of the individuals, are to have all avenues of assisted lifting before work commences. Project leads are to plan to the best of their ability to allow for greater numbers of employees present when heavier doors are to be fitted. Any site where insufficient lifting numbers are present, are to be reported to Head of Installation before work commences.

IMPORTANT NOTE: - Kingsway Group would likewise request any equipment such as fixing of our control panels, 3rd parties are duly mindful and avoid damaging Kingsway equipment, when in situ. They must take into consideration our installation, if / when, they are working in areas where our equipment has been installed and fitted.

FIXING OF DOORS & SECONDARY NOTES

The Door Sets will be off loaded from the vehicle via tail lift and transported by pallet truck to the designated lifting area as agreed with – site Principal Contractor/Client. Prior to the removal of any weather protection / boarding etc, Kingsway Group engineers will set the door in position. This will be done by sharing the load between two or more persons (depending on all up weight of each door and the persons ability) and secured into position by 18v drills and appropriate fixings. Once the door is in place final fixings and connections will be made.

For items such as door furniture, they are easily protected as they are door mounted. Even so care by others should be observed. Kingsway Group Personnel will not carryout 2nd/final fix of their equipment until final finishes to the local areas have been carried out. e.g. walls painted or mist coated, plastering completed. If Kingsway Group are requested to carryout 2nd/final fix equipment prior to the local areas having final finished completed, this will not be at Kingsway Group risk and if replacement equipment is required due to damage from either contact or paint etc. This will be chargeable.

C. Vertical lift zones

Observe the vertical positions of the workers’ hands at both the start and end of the lift. The effect of stature differences between team members is particularly important when lifting goes above elbow height. Record the ‘worst-case’ colour band/score.

D. Torso twisting and sideways bending

Observe the workers’ torsos as they lift the load. If their torsos twist in relation to their hips OR they lean to one side as the load is lifted, the colour band is amber and the score is 1. If their torsos twist AND bend to the side as they lift the load, the colour band is red and the score is 2.

E. Postural constraints

Look for factors that force the team members to modify their postures. If their movements are restricted because of the space available (eg lifting in a narrow aisle or in a crowded or disorganised storage area) or lifting round obstructions, the colour band is amber and the score is 1. If the postures are severely restricted (eg lifting or carrying in an area with a low ceiling) the colour band is red and the score is 3.
